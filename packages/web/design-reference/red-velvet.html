Red Velvet Protein Cookies (Crumbl Base)

5668808e · main · branched Jun 18 · untested TO‑MAKE bake‑off · vs Oat Base crumblcookieproteinred-velvet

Red velvet on the proven Crumbl base chassis — the same dough behind Birthday Cake, Lemon, and Lucky Charms — with cocoa, a little apple cider vinegar for the buttermilk tang, and red gel color. The frosting pin is swapped from the base's default vanilla to the Protein Cream-Cheese Frosting. Leavening is left exactly as the base's (baking powder, no soda) so the tested texture is unchanged — the vinegar is for tang, not lift. Cocoa dries the dough, so lean on the base's add-the-almond-milk-to-loosen step. The Crumbl-base branch of a two-way bake-off: bake it against the Oat Base version, then promote the winner.

Method

Component — make ahead

Protein Cream-Cheese Frosting · 342 cal
Beat the Greek yogurt, low-fat cream cheese, vanilla protein, and Splenda smooth. Add the SF pudding mix to thicken, or chill 20-30 min before using. Keep cold until the cookies are fully cooled.

The cookie

1Mix the dry ingredients into a bowl — flour, cocoa, vanilla protein, Splenda, baking powder, salt.
2Add the wet ingredients — egg, egg white, melted butter, Greek yogurt, almond milk, vanilla, the red gel coloring, and the vinegar — and mix to a dough. Cocoa makes this drier, so add the almond milk 1 tsp at a time until it just comes together.
3Chill 20-30 min, then scoop onto a lined sheet, wet hands, and flatten (this dough barely spreads).
4350°F · ~9 minBake — pull while the centers still look soft; they firm up as they cool.
5Cool the cookies fully, then frost with the protein cream-cheese frosting.
